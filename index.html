Important Announcement

Regarding the Course Grade Improvement Policy

IITM BS Degree Program

Understanding the Previous Policy

What was "Improvement"?

The improvement option was for students who passed a course but were not satisfied with their grade. It allowed them to retake the course to achieve a better score and improve their overall CGPA.

Key Feature of the Old System

The main characteristic of the previous policy was its flexibility. Students could decide to improve a course grade at any point during their degree program, regardless of their current level.

For example, a student who had already advanced to the Degree Level could still choose to retake a Foundation Level course in which they had received a low grade.

New Policy Update: Effective August 28, 2025

New Grade Improvement Policy

As per the new policy, students can improve the grades obtained in courses of a level only as long as they are within the same level and have not progressed to the next.

Level Constraint

A student will not be allowed to take an improvement course from a lower level once they start the next level.

CGPA Freeze

The CGPA will be frozen once a student moves to a new level.

Fees & Requirements

The fee will be twice the regular course fee, and the learner must redo all coursework.

Final Score Calculation

The highest score amongst all attempts will be used for calculating the final CGPA.
